During my second year of highschool, I was lucky enough to be given the opportunity to take an entry test for an early college program at Montgomery College. I was under the impression that this was meant I would be taking Montgomery College classes at my highschool similar to dual enrollment, but much to my surprise, after passing the test I was told that I would now be attending Montgomery College full time! After getting over the initial surprise, the summer of my highschool sophomore year I went to Montgomery College to get prepared for what would be the upcoming semester. The major I had chosen before entry was Computer Science, so that was the program I prepared for during my summer there. When fall came around, I was attending Montgomery College as a 15 year old. While there I was taking both computer science focused classes, as well as classes to substitute for the classes I would be missing at my highschool. Getting thrust into the college experience so early was certainly a little bit nerve-racking, however in hindsight it was a great learning and character building experience, and I graduated May 2021 with both a highschool degree AND an Associates Degree from Montgomery College, there from which I transfered to University of Maryland Baltimore College.

After my semesters at Montgomery College, I transfered to University of Maryland Baltimore County, which is where I am currently at. I decided to continue under the Computer Science major, as by this point I had gotten used to it and wanted to continue after school to a career in the field. My time at a "real" university (a place with a more real university feeling) has been very enjoyable, as has been the student and staff body at this school. The early college program has sped up the degree process, making everything feel like its happening at once and the uptick in course difficulty was noticable, but of course, I persevered and as of right now I am on track to graduate with a Bachelors in Computer Science in May of 2023 as a 19 year old.
